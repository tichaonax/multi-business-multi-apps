## Overview

### Tutorial Series Goals

**Purpose:** Create a comprehensive video library that enables:
- Self-paced employee learning
- Quick reference for specific tasks
- Onboarding support
- Reduced training time
- Consistent knowledge transfer

**Target Audience:**
- New employees (primary)
- Existing staff learning new features
- Managers training their teams
- System administrators

**Video Specifications:**
- **Duration:** 5-10 minutes per tutorial
- **Format:** MP4, 1080p (1920√ó1080)
- **Frame Rate:** 30 fps
- **Audio:** Clear narration with background music (optional)
- **Captions:** Closed captions for accessibility

---

## Production Guidelines

### Equipment Needed

**Essential:**
- Screen recording software (OBS Studio, Camtasia, or ScreenFlow)
- Microphone (USB condenser mic recommended)
- Computer with demo system access
- Script/teleprompter

**Optional:**
- Video editing software (DaVinci Resolve, Adobe Premiere)
- Webcam (for picture-in-picture narration)
- Lighting (for webcam shots)

---

### Recording Best Practices

**Before Recording:**
1. ‚òê Review script thoroughly
2. ‚òê Set up demo environment with fresh data
3. ‚òê Close unnecessary applications
4. ‚òê Disable notifications
5. ‚òê Test microphone levels
6. ‚òê Clear browser cache (for clean demo)
7. ‚òê Prepare demo credentials
8. ‚òê Rehearse workflow

**During Recording:**
1. ‚òê Speak clearly and at moderate pace
2. ‚òê Pause between major steps
3. ‚òê Move mouse deliberately (not too fast)
4. ‚òê Highlight important UI elements
5. ‚òê Allow time for viewers to read text
6. ‚òê Keep cursor movements smooth

**After Recording:**
1. ‚òê Review footage for errors
2. ‚òê Edit out long pauses or mistakes
3. ‚òê Add captions
4. ‚òê Include intro/outro
5. ‚òê Test video playback
6. ‚òê Export in proper format

---

### Video Structure Template

**Standard Format:**
1. **Intro (30 seconds)**
   - Title screen
   - Topic introduction
   - Learning objectives
   - Estimated time

2. **Content (4-8 minutes)**
   - Step-by-step demonstration
   - Real-time narration
   - Visual highlights
   - On-screen annotations

3. **Summary (30-60 seconds)**
   - Recap key points
   - Next steps
   - Related tutorials
   - Call to action

4. **Outro (15 seconds)**
   - Thank you message
   - Support contact info
   - Series branding

---

### Visual Elements

**On-Screen Annotations:**
- Text callouts for important points
- Arrows/highlights for UI elements
- Zoom-in for small details
- Step numbers (1, 2, 3...)
- Keyboard shortcuts displayed

**Branding:**
- Company logo (corner watermark)
- Consistent color scheme
- Standard intro/outro
- Tutorial numbering

## Tutorial 1: Getting Started - First Login

**Duration:** 6 minutes
**Target Audience:** All new employees
**Prerequisites:** None

---

### Script

**[INTRO - 30 seconds]**

> [Title Screen: "Tutorial 1: Getting Started - First Login"]
>
> Welcome to the Multi-Business Management System training series. I'm [Name], and in this tutorial, you'll learn how to log in to the system for the first time, change your password, and navigate the main dashboard.
>
> This tutorial takes about 6 minutes. Let's get started!

---

**[SECTION 1: First Login - 2 minutes]**

> **Step 1: Access the System**
>
> Open your web browser and navigate to the system URL provided by your manager. For this demo, we're using localhost:8080.
>
> [Action: Type URL in browser, press Enter]
>
> You'll see the login screen. This is where you'll enter your credentials every time you access the system.

> **Step 2: Enter Credentials**
>
> You should have received an email with your login credentials. Let me show you where to find them.
>
> [Action: Show example email on screen]
>
> Your email will look something like this. It includes your email address and a temporary password.
>
> For this demonstration, I'll use the demo account:
> - Email: michael.chen@restaurant-demo.com
> - Password: Demo@123
>
> [Action: Type email and password, highlight each field]
>
> Notice that as I type the password, it's hidden for security. This protects your account if someone is watching your screen.

> **Step 3: First Login**
>
> Click the "Sign In" button.
>
> [Action: Click Sign In]
>
> Because this is your first login, the system will immediately prompt you to change your password. This is a security requirement - you must create a unique password that only you know.

---

**[SECTION 2: Password Change - 1.5 minutes]**

> **Step 4: Create New Password**
>
> [Action: Show password change dialog]
>
> You'll see three fields:
> - Current Password: Enter the temporary password from your email
> - New Password: Create a strong, unique password
> - Confirm Password: Type the same password again
>
> [Action: Fill in fields, with password requirements shown]
>
> Your password must meet these requirements:
> - At least 8 characters
> - Include uppercase and lowercase letters
> - Include at least one number
> - Include at least one special character
>
> For this demo, I'll create a password that meets all requirements.
>
> [Action: Type password, show strength indicator]
>
> Notice the password strength indicator turns green when all requirements are met.
>
> Click "Change Password" when ready.
>
> [Action: Click button, show success message]
>
> Great! Your password is now changed. You'll use this new password for all future logins.

---

**[SECTION 3: Dashboard Navigation - 2 minutes]**

> **Step 5: Explore the Dashboard**
>
> [Action: Show main dashboard]
>
> Welcome to your dashboard! This is your home screen. Let me walk you through the main areas.
>
> [Action: Highlight navigation menu on left]
>
> On the left, you'll see the main navigation menu. The items visible depend on your role. Since I'm logged in as a sales associate, I can see:
> - Dashboard (home)
> - Point of Sale
> - Products
> - Customers
> - My Orders
> - My Payroll
>
> [Action: Hover over each menu item]
>
> If you're a manager, you'll see additional options like Reports, Inventory, and Administration.

> **Step 6: Customize Your Dashboard**
>
> [Action: Click on dashboard widgets]
>
> Your dashboard can be customized with widgets showing important information. Here you can see:
> - Your recent sales
> - Daily sales totals
> - Pending tasks
> - Announcements
>
> [Action: Demonstrate adding a widget]
>
> To add a widget, click the "Add Widget" button, select what you want to see, and it will appear on your dashboard. You can drag and drop to rearrange them.

> **Step 7: Update Your Profile**
>
> [Action: Click user menu in top right]
>
> In the top right corner, click on your name to open the user menu. Here you can:
> - View your profile
> - Access settings
> - View notifications
> - Log out
>
> [Action: Click "My Profile"]
>
> Let's update your profile. You can add:
> - Profile photo
> - Phone number
> - Emergency contact
> - Communication preferences
>
> [Action: Show profile editing]
>
> Make any updates you need, then click "Save Changes."

---

**[SUMMARY - 30 seconds]**

> **Recap**
>
> Great job! You've now completed your first login. Let's recap what we covered:
>
> 1. ‚úì Logged in with temporary credentials
> 2. ‚úì Changed your password
> 3. ‚úì Explored the main dashboard
> 4. ‚úì Updated your profile
>
> You're now ready to start learning the core features of the system.
>
> **Next Steps:**
> - Watch Tutorial 2: Processing Your First Sale
> - Familiarize yourself with the navigation menu
> - Explore the dashboard widgets
>
> If you have any questions, contact your manager or IT support.

**[OUTRO - 15 seconds]**

> Thank you for watching! For more tutorials, visit our training portal or contact support@company.com.
>
> [End screen with links to related tutorials]

## Tutorial 2: Processing Your First Sale

**Duration:** 8 minutes
**Target Audience:** Sales staff, front-line employees
**Prerequisites:** Tutorial 1 completed

---

### Script

**[INTRO - 30 seconds]**

> [Title Screen: "Tutorial 2: Processing Your First Sale"]
>
> Welcome back! In this tutorial, you'll learn how to process a sale using the Point of Sale system. By the end of this video, you'll be able to add items to the cart, calculate totals, process payment, and print a receipt.
>
> This is one of the most important skills you'll use daily, so take your time and follow along. Let's get started!

---

**[SECTION 1: Navigate to POS - 1 minute]**

> **Step 1: Access Point of Sale**
>
> After logging in, look at the left navigation menu and click "Point of Sale" or "POS."
>
> [Action: Click POS menu item]
>
> The POS interface will open. This is where you'll spend most of your time when helping customers.
>
> [Action: Show POS interface]
>
> Let's take a quick tour of the POS layout:
>
> [Action: Highlight each area as described]
>
> - **Left side:** Product categories and search
> - **Center:** Products for selected category
> - **Right side:** Shopping cart and checkout
> - **Top:** Customer selection and order controls
>
> This layout is designed for speed and efficiency during busy times.

---

**[SECTION 2: Add Items to Cart - 2 minutes]**

> **Step 2: Select Products**
>
> There are three ways to add products to the cart. Let me show you each one.
>
> **Method 1: Category Buttons**
>
> [Action: Click category button "Entrees"]
>
> Click on a category button to see all products in that category. For example, I'll click "Entrees" to see our main dishes.
>
> [Action: Click product "Burger"]
>
> Now click on a product to add it to the cart. I'll select "Burger."
>
> [Action: Show product added to cart on right]
>
> Notice it appears in the cart on the right side with quantity 1 and the price.

> **Method 2: Search Bar**
>
> [Action: Click in search box]
>
> If you know what the customer wants, it's faster to search. Type the product name in the search box.
>
> [Action: Type "fries"]
>
> As you type, matching products appear. Click the one you want.
>
> [Action: Click "French Fries"]
>
> It's immediately added to the cart.

> **Method 3: Barcode Scanner** (if available)
>
> [Action: Show barcode scanner icon]
>
> If your station has a barcode scanner, simply scan the product barcode. The item is automatically added to the cart. This is the fastest method for retail environments.
>
> [Action: Demonstrate scan or manual barcode entry]

> **Step 3: Adjust Quantities**
>
> What if the customer wants more than one of something?
>
> [Action: Click on quantity number in cart]
>
> Click on the quantity number next to the item in the cart. You can:
> - Type a new number
> - Use the + and - buttons
> - Or click the stepper controls
>
> [Action: Change quantity to 2]
>
> I'll change the fries to quantity 2. Notice the line total updates automatically.

> **Step 4: Remove Items**
>
> If the customer changes their mind, you can remove items.
>
> [Action: Hover over item to show remove button]
>
> Hover over the item and click the trash icon or X button.
>
> [Action: Remove item]
>
> The item is immediately removed and the total updates.

---

**[SECTION 3: Process Payment - 3 minutes]**

> **Step 5: Review the Cart**
>
> Before processing payment, always review the cart with the customer.
>
> [Action: Show cart summary]
>
> You can see:
> - All items and quantities
> - Individual item prices
> - Subtotal
> - Tax (calculated automatically)
> - Total amount due
>
> Make sure everything is correct before proceeding.

> **Step 6: Choose Payment Method**
>
> [Action: Scroll to payment section]
>
> Below the cart, you'll see payment method buttons:
> - Cash
> - Credit/Debit Card
> - Gift Card
> - Split Payment (multiple methods)
>
> Let's process a cash payment first.

> **Cash Payment**
>
> [Action: Click "Cash" button]
>
> Click the "Cash" button. A payment dialog appears.
>
> [Action: Show payment dialog]
>
> The total amount due is displayed. Enter the amount the customer gave you. Let's say they paid with a $50 bill.
>
> [Action: Type "50"]
>
> The system automatically calculates the change: $50.00 - $37.28 = $12.72
>
> [Action: Show change amount highlighted]
>
> Always count the change back to the customer!

> **Card Payment**
>
> [Action: Click "Back" then start new sale for card demo]
>
> For a card payment, add items to a new cart...
>
> [Action: Add items, click "Card" button]
>
> Click the "Card" button. The payment terminal will prompt the customer to insert, tap, or swipe their card.
>
> [Action: Show processing indicator]
>
> Wait for the approval message. This usually takes 3-5 seconds.
>
> [Action: Show approval message]
>
> When you see "Payment Approved," the transaction is complete.

> **Split Payment**
>
> [Action: Demonstrate split payment]
>
> Sometimes customers want to use multiple payment methods. Click "Split Payment" to divide the total.
>
> [Action: Show split payment interface]
>
> You can specify amounts for each payment type:
> - $25 cash
> - $12.28 on card
>
> Process each payment one at a time until the balance is zero.

---

**[SECTION 4: Complete Sale - 1.5 minutes]**

> **Step 7: Print Receipt**
>
> [Action: Show receipt options]
>
> After payment is approved, you have options:
> - Print Customer Receipt
> - Print Business Copy
> - Email Receipt
> - No Receipt
>
> Most customers want a printed receipt. Click "Print Customer Receipt."
>
> [Action: Click button, show print dialog]
>
> The receipt will print automatically if a printer is configured. Otherwise, you can save as PDF or email it.
>
> [Action: Show sample receipt]
>
> The receipt includes:
> - Business name and contact info
> - Order number
> - Date and time
> - Itemized list
> - Tax breakdown
> - Total and payment method
> - Thank you message

> **Step 8: Start New Sale**
>
> [Action: Show "New Sale" button]
>
> After completing a transaction, the cart automatically clears, and you're ready for the next customer.
>
> Click "New Sale" to start fresh if needed.

---

**[SECTION 5: Quick Tips - 30 seconds]**

> **Pro Tips for Fast Service:**
>
> 1. **Learn keyboard shortcuts:** Press F2 to focus search, Enter to add item
> 2. **Use favorites:** Star frequently-sold items for quick access
> 3. **Pre-calculate:** Tell customers the total while processing payment
> 4. **Keep cart visible:** Customers can see what you're ringing up
> 5. **Double-check before payment:** Mistakes are harder to fix after payment

---

**[SUMMARY - 30 seconds]**

> **Recap**
>
> Excellent! You can now process sales. Let's recap:
>
> 1. ‚úì Navigated to POS interface
> 2. ‚úì Added items three different ways (category, search, barcode)
> 3. ‚úì Adjusted quantities and removed items
> 4. ‚úì Processed cash and card payments
> 5. ‚úì Printed receipts
>
> **Practice Challenge:**
> Process 10 practice sales to build speed and confidence. Aim for under 2 minutes per transaction.
>
> **Next Steps:**
> - Watch Tutorial 3: Product Management Basics
> - Watch Tutorial 4: Customer Management
> - Practice with supervisor before serving real customers

**[OUTRO - 15 seconds]**

> Great job! For questions or more training, contact your manager. Happy selling!
