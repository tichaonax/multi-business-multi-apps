## Sizing Recommendation

| Size | Max Weight | Base Price Positioning | Best For |
|------|-----------|----------------------|----------|
| **Small** | 0.4 kg | Low entry price | Solo light lunch |
| **Medium** | 0.7 kg | Standard price | Everyday lunch / dinner |
| **Large** | 1.0 kg | Premium price | Hungry workers, share plate |

> **Tip:** Price the base at a flat rate that covers your fixed costs (packaging, sadza/rice base),
> then let the per-kg rates on each pool item cover the variable protein/vegetable cost.
> Proteins (beef, chicken, fish) should carry a higher $/kg rate than vegetables and soups.

---

## Top Recommendation

**Start with "Mama's Choice"** — medium size, base price around $2.50, and a pool of 6–8 items:

| Pool Item | Why It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| Chicken pieces | Most popular protein |
| Beef stew | Familiar comfort item |
| Fried fish | Affordable protein variety |
| Pumpkin leaves (muboora) | Traditional, low cost, high fill |
| Cabbage relish | Bulk, low cost |
| Bean soup | Vegetarian-friendly, filling |
| Boiled potatoes | Starchy filler, popular |
| Coleslaw | Fresh contrast, appealing colour |

**Why this works operationally:**
- 8 items gives customers real choice without overwhelming the kitchen
- Mix of cheap (veg) and expensive (protein) items at different $/kg lets you stay profitable regardless of what customers pick
- Familiar name ("Mama's") builds trust and repeat purchases quickly

## POS Setup Checklist

- [ ] Create pool items under **Restaurant → AYLI Pool Items** — add emoji for each item
- [ ] Create the combo under **Restaurant → AYLI Combos** — set max weight and max items
- [ ] Add sizes (Small / Medium / Large) with base prices
- [ ] Assign pool items to the combo and set per-kg prices for each size tier
- [ ] Test the combo at the POS — confirm scale tare and weight capture work correctly
- [ ] Verify the customer display shows live weighing progress
