POS — Making a Sale

The 6 Steps of Every Sale

1
Open the POSNavigate to your business → POS. The till opens, ready to take items.
2
Add itemsScan a barcode, type the product name to search, or tap a category then the item.
3
Attach a customerSearch by name / phone, scan their loyalty card, or create a new customer inline. Walk-in is fine — customer is optional.
4
Apply discounts / campaignsEligible campaign rewards are shown automatically. Tap to apply. Enter a manual discount if authorised.
5
Select payment methodCash, card, mobile transfer, layby, or split payment.
6
Complete — print receiptReceipt prints with item lines, change due, and WiFi credentials if WiFi was included.

POS — Barcode Scanner

What Does Scanning Actually Do?

The scanner is always active. One scan can do four different things depending on what the code belongs to.

#If the barcode is…Result
1 An employee card Clocks that employee in or out immediately
2 A customer loyalty card Attaches that customer to the open sale
3 A product barcode Adds that product to the cart at its price
4 Unknown barcode Offers to search other businesses or create a new product

📌 Priority is fixed: Employee → Customer → Product → Unknown. The scanner stops at the first match. No configuration needed — it is automatic.

POS — WiFi Token Sales

Selling WiFi at the Till

Two WiFi systems supported

SystemHow it works
ESP32Captive portal — sells a token code. Customer enters code on WiFi login page.
R710 (Ruckus)Pre-generated username + password pairs sold directly at POS.

R710 tokens are pre-generated and stored in a pool. When stock is below 5, staff see a "Request 5 More" button. Tokens are never generated on the fly.

WiFi in a Combo

A combo product (e.g. "Meal + WiFi") can include a WiFi token. When sold, the receipt prints the food items and the WiFi credentials as a separate block:

Cash Office — End of Day Close

6 Steps to Close the Day

1
Open End of Day pageGo to Cash Office → End of Day for today's date.
2
Rent transfer (if configured)System calculates the daily rent portion. Confirm or skip. This moves funds from the business account to the rent account.
3
Auto-deposits runConfigured recurring deposits (e.g. savings, sinking funds) are processed automatically. If balance is zero the deposit is skipped.
4
Manager sign-offManager types their name and enters total physical cash counted in the till.
5
Save & LockDay's transactions are locked — no more edits. Sales figures are frozen.
6
Cash allocationCashier reviews each deposit line, ticks it off, locks the allocation. Physical cash is counted out per line.

Cash Office — Catch-Up

Closing Multiple Days at Once

If 2–60 days were missed, the manager can run a Grouped EOD Catch-Up instead of closing one day at a time.

RoleWhat they do
Manager Selects the unclosed days, previews combined sales totals, enters name + total cash for the whole period, clicks Run Catch-Up
Cashier Reviews the cash allocation report (all days combined), ticks each deposit line, locks the allocation

⚠️ In a catch-up, the cashier is reconciling cash for multiple days at once. The total entered by the manager is the combined physical cash for all selected days.

Manager — Payment Requests

Payment Request Workflow

1
SubmittedStaff submits a payment request — payee, amount, purpose, account. Manager's bell 🔔 turns red.
2
Reviewed & ApprovedManager opens the request, checks details, clicks Approve. Submitter's bell 🔔 notifies them of approval.
3
Payment MadeCashier / Finance pays the supplier (cash or bank). They open the request and click Mark as Paid with the payment reference.
4
ClosedRequest is closed. The expense account balance is reduced. Original submitter receives a final notification.

💡 You can also decline a request at step 2 with a reason. The submitter is notified and can revise and resubmit.

HR & Employee Management

Contracts, clock-in cards, absences, per diem, and employee lifecycle.

HR — Employee Lifecycle

From Hire to Exit

📝

1. Onboarding

Create employee record → capture personal details, ID, bank account, job title, department. Create employment contract with duties, salary, and type (full-time / part-time / contract). Print contract, upload signed copy, approve.

🖨️

2. Clock-In Card

Print the employee's barcode ID card. Card has name, photo (front) and unique barcode (back). Fold-in-half, laminate, give to employee. They scan it to clock in and out from any screen in the system.

💻

3. System Access

If the employee needs to log into the system, create a system user account for them. Assign permissions from the role-based list (POS, HR, Payroll, Finance, etc.). One account can serve multiple roles.

🔴

4. Termination

Complete final payroll → print payslip + P45 → revoke system access → mark employee as terminated → close expense items → collect property. Leave salary increases and contract history on file.

HR — Attendance & Absences

Clock-In & Absence Tracking

How clock-in works

  • Employee scans barcode card → clock-in recorded instantly
  • Photo is captured with clock-in (optional but tracked)
  • Second scan = clock-out
  • Manager can add or edit entries manually (even back-dated)
  • ⚠ flag = missing clock-out / ✏ flag = manually edited

Absence types & payroll impact

TypePayroll
Annual LeavePaid (from balance)
Sick LeavePaid if balance allows
Unpaid LeaveDeducted pro-rata
AWOLDeducted, disciplinary
Public HolidayPaid, no balance used

Absences deduct automatically at next payroll period. No manual adjustment needed.

Finance — Expenses, Rent & Loans

Finance — Expense Accounts

Two Types of Expense Accounts

🏢

Business Expense Account

Shared account for a specific business purpose. Funded by deposits from the business account. Payments made via the Payment Request workflow. Manager can see all transactions.

Examples: Grocery Procurement, Utilities, Maintenance, Vehicle Fuel

🔒

Personal Expense Account

Private to the account holder. Used by an individual to track their own spending and submit reimbursement claims. Manager cannot see the transactions unless the holder shares them.

Example: Staff member tracks meals, transport, stationery bought personally, then claims back.

💡 Petty Cash uses an expense account as its fund. Requests go through the same approval chain (Submit → Approve → Pay → Close). Unspent cash must be returned and recorded.

Finance — Rent & Loans

Rent Account & Loan Systems

Rent Account

  • Set up once: monthly rent, operating days, landlord, due date
  • System calculates daily transfer amount automatically
  • Each EOD close prompts a rent transfer to the rent account
  • At month end, one bulk payment from rent account to landlord
  • Balance bar shows % of month's rent funded (🟢 100% / 🟠 75% / 🔴 low)

Two loan systems

TypeDirectionExample
Business LoanLender → BusinessBank loan, investor advance
Employee LoanBusiness → EmployeeSalary advance

Employee loans can auto-deduct via Payroll Deduction — no manual repayment tracking needed.

Customers, Loyalty & Campaigns

Customer numbers, loyalty cards, activity reports, and the campaigns & rewards system.

Customers

Loyalty Cards & Campaigns

Loyalty Card

  • Every customer gets a unique number: RES-CUST-000001
  • Printed as a barcode on a fold-in-half card
  • Cashier scans the card — customer is attached to the sale in 1 second
  • Card number never changes, even if name / phone is updated
  • Customer activity report: total spend, visits, campaign rewards earned

Campaigns & Rewards

  • Manager creates a campaign with a spend threshold (e.g. spend $50, get a reward)
  • At month end: click Generate Monthly Rewards — system finds all qualifying customers and issues reward vouchers
  • At POS: eligible reward appears automatically when customer is scanned — cashier taps to apply
  • Reward statuses: Issued → Redeemed / Expired

Inventory & Barcode Labels

Creating new products, restocking existing ones, barcode assignment, and printing labels.

Inventory — Two Workflows

Create New vs Receive Existing Stock

WorkflowWhen to useWhat it does
Create New Product Product has never been in the system before Creates a new product record, assigns a barcode, sets initial stock quantity
Receive Stock Product exists — you just got more units Adds quantity to the existing product — does not create a duplicate

⚠️ Always search for the product first. Creating a duplicate product causes stock split — the same item exists twice, making reports wrong.

3 ways to assign a barcode

1. Type/scan directly on the product form
2. Assign after saving via the barcode modal
3. Bulk auto-generate with a prefix (clothing)

After assigning — print labels

Barcode Management → Templates → New Template → From Existing Product → set label size → Print Job → print quantity

Inventory — Scanning Unknown Barcodes

What Happens When You Scan an Unknown Item?

1
Check this businessBarcode found → product added to cart. Done.
2
Check barcode templatesTemplate found → pre-filled product form opens. One click to create.
3
Search all other businessesBarcode found in Restaurant / Grocery / Hardware → shows the match, offers to stock it here.
4
Completely unknown → Quick Stock AddForm opens with barcode pre-filled. Enter name, price, quantity. Product created immediately.

📌 The same barcode can be a different product in different businesses — that is fine. Each business owns its own barcode→product mapping. There is no conflict.

Inventory — Clothing & Templates

Bales & Barcode Templates

Used Clothing Bales

  • A bale is a bulk lot of second-hand items (e.g. 200 pieces at $0.80 each)
  • Record the bale: item count, cost price, unit selling price
  • Print a barcode label per bale (e.g. BALE-HXI-042)
  • Staff scan the bale barcode at POS — one item is sold at unit price, count decrements
  • BOGO promotions can be configured per bale
  • View remaining count and cost recovery per bale

Barcode Templates

  • A template saves a barcode + default product data (name, price, size, colour, SKU)
  • Created once per product line at Barcode Management → Templates
  • When a new batch arrives, scan the barcode → template is found → one click creates the product with all fields pre-filled
  • Configure label size, font, symbology (CODE128, EAN13, etc.), and paper format (A4, label_2x1, CR80)

Section 20 — Team Chat

Built-In Team Chat

How to open it

  • Click the 💬 Chat button in the sidebar — from any screen
  • A floating panel opens — draggable, stays on top
  • All staff share one General room
  • Messages delivered in real time via WebSocket

🔴 Unread badge appears on the chat button when a message arrives while the panel is closed. Also sends a 🔔 bell notification.

What to use it for

  • Shift handover notes
  • Quick questions to manager or cashier
  • Closing reminders and briefings
  • Coordination between tills

⚠️ Messages auto-delete after 7 days. Do not use chat to record financial data or decisions — use payroll notes, expense account notes, or contract records instead.

Summary

Key Takeaways

📟

One scanner, many actions

Employee card → clock in. Customer card → attach to sale. Product barcode → add to cart. Unknown → create product.

🔔

Bell = action needed

Never ignore a red bell. Every pending approval, payment, or payroll sits in the notification panel waiting for you.

🔒

EOD locks are permanent

Once a day is locked, it cannot be changed. Count carefully before locking. Batch catch-up is available for missed days.

👔

Owner must approve payroll

No payroll spreadsheet can be exported without owner approval. The status chain is strictly one-way.

📦

Create vs Restock

Create a new product only if it has never been in the system. For existing products, use Receive Stock to add quantity.
